# Acknowledgements

This work originated from an effort to develop a principled approach to
multi-scale signal analysis grounded in first principles. By examining
the behavior of aggregation at its most elementary level, the
divisibility structure underlying admissible window families emerges
naturally, and the corresponding computational method follows.

The interpretation of this structure as a geometric measurement space
reflects the author's longstanding engagement with number-theoretic
representations of integers through their prime factorizations, making
the use of p-adic valuation as a coordinate system immediate.

The resulting framework can be viewed as a finite analogue of Riemann
integration, in which the grain defines the finest admissible partition
and no limiting process is required.
